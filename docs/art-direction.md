## The three directions

Each direction is a complete visual system. They differ not in taste but in **what emotion the site produces on first scroll**. Interior design is emotional real estate — pick the mood that matches the studio's voice.

---

### Direction A — Editorial Warm

**One-line:** *A Kinfolk magazine spread you can scroll through.*

**Mood:** Slow, considered, sunlit. The site feels like flipping through a printed portfolio in a quiet room. Confident but not loud. Sophisticated but never cold.

**Materiality (photography feel):** Natural daylight, warm shadows, linen and oak textures, hand-thrown ceramics, framed art hung slightly imperfect. Interiors that look *lived in*, not staged.

**Contrast level:** Low-to-medium. Off-white surfaces, warm neutrals, no harsh blacks. Text sits on warm cream, not stark white.

**Color palette direction:**
- Background: warm ivory `#F5EFE7` or cream
- Surface: dusty beige `#EAE0D2`
- Text: warm charcoal `#2A2724` (never true black)
- Accent: burnt terracotta, deep clay, or forest green — chosen from Phase 1's 3 options
- Muted: warm taupe `#8A7F72`

**Typography feel:** Serif display for headlines (editorial magazine feel — think **Freight Display**, **Canela**, **GT Sectra**), paired with a clean humanist sans for body (**Söhne**, **Inter**, or free: **Geist**).

**Motion feel:** Slow, unhurried. Long fades, gentle parallax, generous whitespace between reveals. Nothing snappy — the site breathes.

**Best fit if:** The studio does residential interiors, boutique hotels, or restoration projects. Clients who value craft, patina, and storytelling.

**Reference sites (screenshot these for feel):**
- [studiomcgee.com](https://studiomcgee.com)
- [athenacalderone.com](https://athenacalderone.com)
- [heidicaillierdesign.com](https://heidicaillierdesign.com)
- [kellywearstler.com](https://kellywearstler.com)

**Do:** off-white surfaces, serif headlines, warm photography, wide-leading text
**Don't:** pure black text, aggressive animations, cold blues, geometric sans-serifs

### Direction B — Minimal Nordic

**One-line:** *A gallery. The design steps back so the photography can speak.*

**Mood:** Quiet, precise, disciplined. The site itself is nearly invisible — the interiors do the talking. Every pixel earns its place. This is the direction closest in spirit to Makhno, but colder.

**Materiality:** Cool daylight, high-key photography, concrete, brushed steel, blonde wood, plaster walls, minimal styling. Interiors that look like they were *just finished* — pristine.

**Contrast level:** High. Cool white surfaces, black text, precise separation. Photography carries most of the color.

**Color palette direction:**
- Background: pure cool white `#FAFAFA`
- Surface: pale gray `#EEEEED`
- Text: near-black `#0A0A0A`
- Accent: single restrained hit — deep steel blue, matte black, or oxblood — used sparingly
- Muted: cool gray `#8C8C8C`

**Typography feel:** Sans-serif everything. Precise, geometric, wide letter-spacing on headings (**Söhne**, **Neue Haas Grotesk**, or free: **Geist Sans**). Small caps for section labels.

**Motion feel:** Precise, disciplined. Quick fades, small parallax, exact timing. Nothing decorative — every animation reveals content, never for show.

**Best fit if:** The studio does high-end commercial, hospitality, or minimalist residential. Clients who value restraint and precision.

**Reference sites:**
- [normarchitects.com](https://normarchitects.com)
- [vincentvanduysen.com](https://vincentvanduysen.com)
- [studioilse.com](https://studioilse.com)
- [johnpawson.com](https://johnpawson.com)

**Do:** cool whites, sans-serif, generous negative space, tight image grids, single restrained accent
**Don't:** warm creams, serifs, decorative animations, multiple accent colors

---

### Direction C — Moody Sculptural

**One-line:** *A cinematic reel. Dramatic light, deep shadows, materials that beg to be touched.*

**Mood:** Cinematic, sensual, immersive. The site feels closer to a film title sequence than a portfolio. Dark surfaces punctuated by lit photography. Confidence bordering on theatrical.

**Materiality:** Directional light, deep shadows, chiaroscuro. Blackened steel, dark walnut, unpolished marble, raw plaster, patinated brass. Interiors shot at dusk or with dramatic single-source lighting.

**Contrast level:** Extreme. Photography sits in near-darkness, punctuated by warm highlights. Text is warm off-white on rich charcoal or deep bronze.

**Color palette direction:**
- Background: rich charcoal `#1A1815` (warm-toned dark, not cool)
- Surface: deep bronze `#2B2620`
- Text: warm off-white `#EDE6DA`
- Accent: molten amber, oxblood, or aged copper
- Muted: warm gray-brown `#6B6259`

**Typography feel:** Bold serif or slab for headlines (drama), paired with a compact sans for body. Think **PP Editorial Old**, **Canela Deck**, **GT Alpina** — with **Geist Mono** for tags/labels.

**Motion feel:** Cinematic. Dramatic reveals with heavier easing, longer holds, image scaling on hover, WebGL distortion becomes central rather than decorative. Custom cursor is present, glowing subtly.

**Best fit if:** The studio does luxury hospitality, high-end residential with heavy materials, or wants to compete with global-tier award-winning studios (this is closest to Makhno's own mood).

**Reference sites:**
- [makhnostudio.com](https://makhnostudio.com) (the reference)
- [studiokoinc.com](https://studiokoinc.com)
- [guillaumealan.com](https://guillaumealan.com)
- [pierreyovanovitch.com](https://pierreyovanovitch.com)

**Do:** rich darks, dramatic photography, heavy typography, cinematic pacing, WebGL used boldly
**Don't:** warm ivories, cool whites, sunny photography, minimal restraint

## How to pick

Ask yourself: **"When someone lands on my homepage, what should they feel in the first 3 seconds?"**

- Warmth and craft → **A**
- Restraint and precision → **B**
- Drama and materiality → **C**

There is no wrong choice — only the wrong choice for the studio's voice.

---

## What happens after you pick

1. The chosen direction becomes the reference in every subsequent decision.
2. Phase 1 opens with **3 accent color options** and **3 display font options** — all tuned to your chosen direction.
3. `docs/reference/moodboards/<direction>/` gets populated with visual references (you can add images you like there too).
4. The direction is locked; changing it later means re-doing Phase 1.
